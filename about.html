About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Department of Astronomy and Steward Observatory at the University of Arizona (Tucson, AZ). I received my PhD in December 2024 from the University of Groningen in the Netherlands, where I worked at the Kapteyn Astronomical Institute under the supervision of Karina Caputi. Before that, I earned my Master’s degree at the University of Bologna in Italy, where I worked with Andrea Cimatti and Michele E. M. Moresco on simulations of the Near Infrared Spectrometer and Photometer data from Euclid, specifically estimating the expected number counts of Lyman-alpha emitters in the Euclid Deep Fields.

I have a strong interest in the high-redshift Universe (z ≳ 2), to understand how galaxies formed and evolved across cosmic time. To this end, I use imaging and spectroscopy from space-based observatories such as the HST and the JWST. My research focuses on star-forming galaxies and active galactic nuclei. Currently, I am particularly focused on the population of extremely red, compact galaxies unveiled by JWST in the early Universe, commonly referred to as Little Red Dots, whose nature and evolution are still not fully understood. My interests are broader, though, as I am also interested in large-scale structure formation, with ongoing efforts focused on studying proto-clusters and the galaxy populations embedded within them.

My expertise spans a broad range of observational techniques and data analysis methods, including ultraviolet, optical, and infrared astronomy; construction and interpretation of multi-wavelength catalogues; spectral energy distribution fitting; and advanced image processing and data reduction. I work extensively with JWST instruments, including NIRCam, MIRI, NIRISS, and NIRSpec, in both imaging and spectroscopic modes.

Finally, I am an official associate member of both the European and U.S. JWST/MIRI GTO teams focused on high-redshift science. I actively contribute to the JADES, SMILES, MIDIS, and OASIS collaborations and serve as co-PI of the MIRIC team, dedicated to MIRI imaging in two lensing clusters (MACS J0416 and Abell 2744).
