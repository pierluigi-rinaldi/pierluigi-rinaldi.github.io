About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Department of Astronomy and Steward Observatory at the University of Arizona (Tucson, AZ). I received my PhD in December 2024 from the University of Groningen in the Netherlands, where I worked at the Kapteyn Astronomical Institute under the supervision of Karina Caputi. Before that, I earned my Master’s degree at the University of Bologna in Italy, where I worked with Andrea Cimatti and Michele E. M. Moresco.

My research focuses on galaxy formation and evolution at high redshift (z ≳ 2), using imaging and spectroscopy from HST and JWST. I study star-forming galaxies, AGN, and, more recently, the compact, extremely red sources known as Little Red Dots. I also investigate large-scale structure formation, with ongoing work on proto-clusters and their galaxy populations.

My expertise covers UV to IR astronomy, multi-wavelength catalogues, SED fitting, and advanced image processing. I work extensively with JWST’s NIRCam, MIRI, NIRISS, and NIRSpec, in both imaging and spectroscopy.

I am an associate member of the European and U.S. JWST/MIRI GTO teams and co-PI of the MIRIC team, focused on MIRI imaging in MACS J0416 and Abell 2744. I also contribute to JADES, SMILES, MIDIS, and OASIS.
